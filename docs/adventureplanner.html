The Challenge

Through contextual background research, my team and I uncovered a flurry of confusion in the way people conduct research for travel and plan their trips. By analyzing the different players in the game who sought to make travel planning easy, we saw tangible challenges everyday users were facing. The main weak points we identified were:

  • Users have to jump between a lot of different tools/resources in order to holistically plan a trip (not much consolidation).
  • Users possess different "travel styles" which current tools are not flexible enough to cater to all different user groups.
  • It's often difficult for users to navigate the information overload and parse through hundreds of recommendations when researching for a trip.

Data Analysis

Through targeted data analysis using Tableau, I was able to see current trends and validate our hypotheses in the problem space.

Think Aloud Study with Skeema

We wanted to see how people used currently available resources and tools for travel planning, so we conducted a think-aloud study with an existing planning tool, Skeema.

Users were tasked with doing preliminary research on a weekend trip, and as they completed the task my team and I were able to pull several impactful insights.

Analysis and Synthesis

After completing several synthesis activities, my team was able to isolate our research goal for the project as :

How might we help travel planners efficiently find travel guidance that is relevant to their needs and preferences?

Contextual Research

In order to answer our guiding questions, we designed a research protocol utilizing contextual inquiry and a survey to gather our answers.

Our goals for the interviews were to understand how and why the participants make specific decisions when it comes to research as well reveal possible environmental factors affecting their travel preferences.
